About Me

Through varying scales and strokes, Gabriella Rudy has developed an alternative method of working with oils, rarely confining herself to a brush. Rudy’s work shows an intense yearning to understand how the struggles of the human experience affect our emotional landscape—starting with her own challenge, when, at age 18, she was involved in an Amtrak train crash. Rudy’s large-scale paintings are charts of human experience, and although initially perceived as wide-reaching, are meant to engage the viewer in a specific emotion without controlling their stream of consciousness. Rudy lives and works in DC, and teaches art therapy classes to underprivileged children, with the hopes of providing a cathartic outlet for expression and a faithful tool for communication.
